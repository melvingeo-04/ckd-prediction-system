About Kidneys

The Body's Natural Filter

Your kidneys filter about 200 litres of blood daily, removing waste and excess fluids. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) occurs when kidneys are damaged and can't filter blood effectively — it develops silently over years, making early screening critical.

About CKD

What is Chronic Kidney Disease?

CKD is a long-term condition where kidneys gradually lose their ability to filter blood. It develops silently over years — symptoms often appear only in later stages when significant damage has occurred.

Why Early Detection Matters

By the time symptoms appear, significant kidney damage may have already occurred. AI-powered early screening allows timely intervention and far better outcomes.
